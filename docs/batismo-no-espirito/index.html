Batismo no Espírito Santo (baptism in the Spirit)

baptism in the Holy Spirit; efusão do Espírito

The Catholic Charismatic Renewal's central experience term, dated by the movement to Duquesne University in February 1967: 'an individual receiving a personal experience of the power of God, as the Apostles did at Pentecost' — an experience distinct from the sacrament of baptism, not a rebaptism. The theologians the movement cites (McDonnell and Montague) frame it as 'integral to Christian initiation' and 'not special grace for some but common grace for all'; the first stated objective of CHARIS, the body the Dicastery for Laity, Family and Life erected in 2018, is 'to help deepen and promote the grace of baptism in the Holy Spirit throughout the Church'. Attributed to the movement's and the dicastery's own wording; assessments of the practice stay in the rcc project.[1] [2]

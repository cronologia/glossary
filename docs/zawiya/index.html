Zawiya

zāwiya, zaouia; cf. khanqah, tekke

A Sufi lodge ('corner, nook' in Arabic): the building or complex where an order teaches, houses travelers and holds its dhikr — functioning variously as oratory, school, hostel and shrine, often around a founder's tomb. It is the usual term in North and West Africa; the Persianate world uses khanqah and the Ottoman-Turkish world tekke for the functionally similar institution.[1] [2]

Integrismo (integrism)

intégrisme, integrism; distinct from the Brazilian integralismo of the 1930s

A label from Catholicism's anti-modernist era: after Pius X's 1907 condemnation of modernism, the militants of 'integral Catholicism' — the position that the Catholic faith should be the basis of public law and public policy, against pluralism and church–state separation — came to be called integristes/integristas; the word had earlier named a Spanish Catholic party of the 1890s. In current French, Spanish and Portuguese use it is largely pejorative: per the Portuguese Wikipedia, a label 'employed by political adversaries to describe groups and individuals who actually self-identify differently, such as traditionalists' — which is how it circulates around the TFP and SSPX milieus. An attributed label, not a self-description; and not to be confused with the Brazilian integralismo (Ação Integralista Brasileira, 1932–37), a separate political movement.[1] [2]

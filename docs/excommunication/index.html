Excommunication

The gravest canonical censure: the excommunicated person is forbidden to celebrate or receive the sacraments and to exercise ecclesiastical offices or functions (can. 1331). Canonists stress it is a medicinal penalty aimed at repentance, not a declaration that someone is 'expelled from the Church' — a nuance both sides of the traditionalist disputes invoke.[1]
